The Logic: Why did I ask Gemini to "act as a skeptical VC" ?

I utilized a Skeptical Agent persona to counteract AI sycophancy. This ensures the final Investment Memo identifies market risks and regulatory hurdles that a standard 'summarization' prompt would miss.

Every project research content has it ups and downs, no matter how refined , no matter how deeply researched , if even a single piece of data misaligns or just doesn't add up not only can it be 
a major financial risk for the company when it comes to implementing measures for betterment , or locking down potential multi-million dollar deals with a client,  the invalid or non-cross referenced data can cost us alot.

In the world of investing and high-level business, "optimism" is cheap. Anyone can ask an AI to say why a company is great. But a Skeptical VC prompt forces the AI to:

1) Kill the "Hype": Most AI models tend to be "agreeable" (sycophancy). By telling it to be skeptical, you force it to look for the "red flags" that could lose a client $15M.

2) Stress-Test the Data: It forces Gemini to cross-reference the Perplexity research. Instead of just accepting a competitor's claim, a skeptical AI asks, "Wait, if they claim 99% accuracy, why are their Reddit reviews complaining about errors?"

3) Prove Your Value: When you show this to a client on LinkedIn or Discord, it proves you understand business reality. It shows you aren't just a "prompt engineer"; you are a strategist who knows that accuracy + caution = high-ticket value.
